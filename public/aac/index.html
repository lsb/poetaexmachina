Avernus at Cumae.
Images: Lee Butterman.
Text: Vergil, tr Robert Fitzgerald.

  The cliff's huge flank is honeycombed,
cut out in a cavern,

 



  perforated a hundred times,
having a hundred mouths,
with rushing voices
carrying the response of the Sybil.

 



  In lonely night they walked on,
darkly, through the gloom,
through the empty houses and lands of Dis,
as when one goes through a wood
by a faint moon's light,
when Jupiter veils the sky,
and dark night blots the colors of the world.

 



  Easy is the descent to Avernus.
Black Dis's door stands open night and day.
But to retrace your steps to heaven's air,
that is the trouble, that is the toil.
